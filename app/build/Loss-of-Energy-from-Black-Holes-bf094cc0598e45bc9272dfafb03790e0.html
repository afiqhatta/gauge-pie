Flux going in and out of the Black Hole

In general relativity, the energy and momentum can be written in a single, chunky object. This object called the energy-momentum tensor, and it can be written as a matrix. If we have 5 coordinates, then the matrix will be a 5 by 5 matrix, where each row represents a coordinate component. This tensor is conserved, and this is expressed in the conservation law. From this, we can define flux term. Flux is a rough word to describe the component of the tensor in a specific direction.

The term above is the electromagnetic energy flux. There is also another angular momentum flux. This is when we isolate the EM tensor component in the axial direction. For the electromagnetic energy flux, we are interested in flux of two directions - the radial direction (which points inwards/outwards from the origin), and the theta coordinate, which moves along lines of longitude. These two coordinates are known as the poloidal components. Explicit terms for the poloidal components for the energy flux are shown below
