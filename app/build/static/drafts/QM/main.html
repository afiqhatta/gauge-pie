1 Introduction
We are already familiar with the notion of a ten-
sor, an multidimensional object which transforms
nicely under a change of coordinate basis. It is in
our interest then, to go one step further and ex-
plore objects which transform nicely under con-
tinuous rotations in some space. These nice
objects are called spinors! In physics, we come
across spinors in quantum ﬁeld theory to moti-
vate the derivation of Dirac’s equation. Spinors
are important because they are geometrical ob-
jects which transform ’nicely’ with regards to ro-
tations in Euclidean space, and with regards to
Lorentz boosts in Minkowski space. More pre-
cisely in the case of 4-dimensional space-time,
if we adjust our frame of reference by Lorentz
