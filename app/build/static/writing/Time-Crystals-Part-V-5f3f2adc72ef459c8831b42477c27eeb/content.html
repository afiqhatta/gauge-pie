The Support of a Quantum Operator

If we have an operator and know the eigenstates, then we can write it out in terms of its eigenstates and eigenvalues.

The support of a function is the region in the domain where the function is non-zero. How does this apply in the quantum context? Well, quantum states are vectors in a Hilbert space. Quantum operators are linear maps that act on this vector space. So if we define the kernel of an operator as the set of vectors that get reduced to zero, then the support is the orthogonal complement of the kernel.

Now that we’ve defined the kernel, we can determine the support. The orthogonal complement of a subspace is the set of all vectors that reduce them to zero when we contract them.
